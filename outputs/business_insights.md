# Business Insights

## 1. Sales are sizeable but profitability needs active monitoring
- **Observation:** The business generated ₹21.70 Cr in sales and ₹3.33 Cr in profit across 4,200 orders.
- **Data evidence:** Overall profit margin is 15.3%.
- **Business interpretation:** The company is growing revenue, but leadership should track profit margin alongside sales because sales volume alone can hide discounting or return-related margin pressure.
- **Recommended action:** Use profit margin as a permanent KPI card in the executive dashboard.

## 2. South is the strongest region by sales
- **Observation:** South has the highest sales among all regions.
- **Data evidence:** South sales are ₹6.47 Cr with profit of ₹99.88 L.
- **Business interpretation:** South appears to be the strongest regional opportunity and can be used as a benchmark for other regions.
- **Recommended action:** Study South's category mix, campaign channels and store/customer behavior to replicate successful practices in lower-performing regions.

## 3. East has the weakest sales base and should be reviewed
- **Observation:** East is the lowest region by sales.
- **Data evidence:** East sales are ₹4.89 Cr compared with South at ₹6.47 Cr.
- **Business interpretation:** East may need better regional marketing, inventory planning or localized offers.
- **Recommended action:** Review campaign spend, stock availability and customer acquisition strategy for East.

## 4. Technology is the main profit driver
- **Observation:** Technology contributes the highest profit.
- **Data evidence:** Technology profit is ₹2.80 Cr from sales of ₹15.39 Cr.
- **Business interpretation:** Technology is a high-impact category for both revenue and profit growth.
- **Recommended action:** Prioritize Technology in campaigns, inventory availability and premium product placement.

## 5. Furniture carries return risk
- **Observation:** Furniture has the highest return rate among major categories.
- **Data evidence:** Furniture return rate is 7.7%.
- **Business interpretation:** High returns may be linked to bulky logistics, product mismatch, quality expectations or delivery delays.
- **Recommended action:** Investigate Furniture return reasons and improve product descriptions, packaging, quality checks and delivery communication.

## 6. Discounting should be controlled carefully
- **Observation:** Discount and profit show a weak-to-negative relationship.
- **Data evidence:** Correlation between discount and profit is -0.27. Orders with 20%+ discount should be reviewed separately.
- **Business interpretation:** Heavy discounts may increase sales volume but can reduce profitability.
- **Recommended action:** Use discount guardrails and monitor discount bucket profitability before expanding aggressive promotions.

## 7. Standard Class has the highest average delivery delay
- **Observation:** Standard Class takes the longest time to deliver.
- **Data evidence:** Standard Class average delivery time is 4.7 days.
- **Business interpretation:** Longer delivery windows can affect customer satisfaction and return behavior.
- **Recommended action:** Track delivery delay buckets and evaluate whether high-value orders should be moved to faster shipping modes.

## 8. Home Office is the largest customer segment by sales but has higher return risk
- **Observation:** Home Office leads sales among customer segments.
- **Data evidence:** Home Office sales are ₹7.45 Cr and return rate is 5.7%.
- **Business interpretation:** This segment is valuable but may need focused retention and return-reduction actions.
- **Recommended action:** Monitor Home Office returns and create segment-specific product recommendations.

## 9. Organic channel is the largest sales contributor
- **Observation:** Organic channel contributes the highest sales.
- **Data evidence:** Organic sales are ₹8.88 Cr.
- **Business interpretation:** The brand has meaningful organic demand, which is valuable because it may have lower acquisition cost.
- **Recommended action:** Continue investing in SEO, product experience and retention while using paid campaigns selectively.
